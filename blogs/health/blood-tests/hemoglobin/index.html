Hemoglobin Test

What is a hemoglobin test?

A hemoglobin test measures the amount of hemoglobin in your blood. Hemoglobin, sometimes abbreviated to Hb or Hgb, is an iron-rich protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen throughout the body.

What is it used for?

This test can be used for multiple purposes. Firstly, it may be ordered as part of a complete blood count to assess overall health. It can also be used to screen for or diagnose certain conditions, such as anemia. The test is also used to monitor existing conditions.

Why do I need a hemoglobin test?

A hemoglobin test can be ordered for a few different reasons. Firstly, your healthcare provider may have ordered this test as part of a complete blood count to assess your overall health. They may also order this test if they suspect you have anemia (not enough red blood cells) or polycythemia vera (too many red blood cells). Symptoms of both these conditions include fatigue, weakness, and dizziness. If you already know you have one of these conditions, a hemoglobin test may be ordered to monitor the condition.

Additionally, this test may be ordered if you have a family history of blood disorders.

What happens during a hemoglobin test?

The site of injection, usually the back of the hand or inside of the elbow, is cleaned with an antiseptic. A needle is inserted into a vein at the site of injection to draw blood. Once the blood sample has been collected, the needle is removed, and the punctured site is covered.

You may return to normal activities once the test is complete.

Will I need to do anything to prepare for the test? If you are only getting your hemoglobin tested, you can eat and drink normally prior to the test. However, this test may be ordered with other tests that require you to fast. As such, it is important that you follow any instructions given to you by your healthcare provider.

What do the results mean?

Low levels of hemoglobin in the blood (anemia) may indicate:

  • Certain cancers, such as leukemia

  • Folate deficiency

  • Hypothyroidism

  • Iron deficiency

  • Kidney disease

  • Liver disease

  • Sickle cell disease

  • Thalassemia

  • Vitamin B12 deficiency High levels of hemoglobin in the blood may indicate:

  • Dehydration

  • Excessive vomiting

  • Heart disease

  • Lung disease

  • Polycythemia vera

Are there any risks to the test?

The risks to this test are minimal. You may experience some temporary bruising or pain at the needle’s site of entry. Less common risks of a blood test include lightheadedness and fainting.

Is there anything else I need to know about a hemoglobin test?

Some types of anemia are mild, while other types are much more severe. If you have been diagnosed with anemia, it is important to speak with your healthcare provider to develop an appropriate treatment plan.

Additionally, if you have abnormal hemoglobin levels, it does not necessarily mean that you have a medical condition that requires treatment. Results can be affected by medications, exercise, diet, one’s menstrual cycle, and more.
